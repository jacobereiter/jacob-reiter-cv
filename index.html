About

I'm a product and research leader working at the intersection of artificial intelligence, neuroscience, and psychiatry. I'm interested in how AI and digital biomarkers can transform how we understand and treat mental illness.

Currently I'm a Group Product Manager at Tempus AI, where I lead the AI Biomarker Platform and the Neurology & Psychiatry portfolio. I also serve on the US Gov. Foundation for the NIH's steering committee for MAP-D, a public-private partnership developing biomarkers for depressive disorders.

Previously, I was Product Lead for Psychiatry AI/ML at Verily (Google Life Sciences), where I led the psychiatry and behavioral health portfolio, including novel AI/ML digital biomarkers, real-world evidence registries for pharmaceutical sponsors, and an EHR-based ML suicidality risk platform. Before Verily, I was an AI/ML researcher at the Stanford Department of Psychiatry, working on deep learning, genomics, and explainable AI applied to psychiatric biomarker discovery.

I'm a part-time PhD candidate in Psychiatry at the University of Cambridge, focused on applied AI and computational psychiatry.

I hold an M.S. in Computer Science (Biocomputation) and a B.S. in Symbolic Systems (AI) from Stanford University.
